Butter Roll

★★★★★

Bread and Biscuits

Prep Time: 1 Source: tomiz.com

Ingredients

200 g Bread Flour

50 g Cake Flour

25 g Granulated Sugar

5 g Guerand's Salt (fine particles)

10 g dry milk

4 g Instant dry yeast

120 g Water

60 g Whole Egg

40 g Unsalted Butter

Directions

All materials are weighed on a digital scale.

[Guideline for the temperature of the water] If the room temperature is 28 ° C or higher, the water temperature is 10 ° C or lower. If the room temperature is around 20 ° C, the water temperature is around 20 ° C. If the room temperature is 10 ° C or lower, use water at about 28 ° C.

Put the flour, salt, sugar in a bowl and mix with a rubber spatula.

Make a hollow well in the middle, add yeast, water and eggs and mix well.

Put it all together until it's no longer powdery in the bowl.

Put the dough on the kneading board.

When kneading, the dough is loose and difficult to put together, so do it while collecting the dough with a drech.

Lift the dough.

Hit the dough on the kneading board, and fold the dough that you are holding onto the hit dough.

Cross the hands 90 degrees and lift the dough.

Repeat steps (6) to (8) to collect the dough.

Put the butter warmed to room temperature and repeat steps (5) to (8).

It is difficult to put together the dough that has started to be mixed with butter, but the dough will be put together by repeating the kneading work.

When you dough pull the dough apart, the dough should stretch like a thin film.

Put in a bowl and allow to rise until about twice the size. (Primary rise: standard 28 ℃ 60 minutes ~)

Take the dough out of the bowl, divide it into 40g pieces, and roll them up. Rise time is 30 minutes. (Can be divided into about 12 pieces)

Tri-fold molding

Crush the dough with your palm.

Fold the bottom third of the dough up and press it down with your palm.

Fold the top third of the dough down and press it down with your palm.

Pull one side to make it narrow.

With the narrow tip of the dough facing down, roll it out from the center down

Place it on baking dish with seams facing down and allow to rise until it is about twice the size. (Secondary rise: 50 minutes at 35 ° C)

Coat with egg.

Baking at 200 ℃ for 10 to 12 minutes.
