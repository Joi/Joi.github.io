Spinach and Olive Focaccia

Bread, Italian

Prep Time: 15min Cook Time: 1h 15min Difficulty: Easy Servings: 6 portions Source: cookidoo.thermomix.com

Ingredients

4 oz extra virgin olive oil, divided, plus extra to serve

3 - 4 oz pitted Sicilian green olives, halved, plus 2 oz olive juice

13 ½ oz water

5 oz fresh baby spinach, de-stemmed

22 oz bread flour

5 tsp active dry yeast

1 tsp salt

1 pinch sugar

2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves only

1 heaping tsp sea salt flakes

Directions

Line a baking pan (14 in. x 16 in. x 2 in.) with parchment and set aside.

Place a bowl onto mixing bowl lid, weigh in 3 oz olive oil and set aside. Place separate bowls onto mixing bow lid, weigh in 2 oz olive juice and weigh 3-4 oz green olive halves in a separate bowl and set aside.

Place water and spinach into mixing bowl and blend 40 sec/speed 10.

Add flour, yeast, 1 tsp salt, 1 oz olive oil and sugar and knead 5 min/.

Tip dough from mixing bowl onto prepared pan, allowing as much dough as possible to fall out, then scrape remaining dough from mixing bowl with spatula. With wet hands, flatten dough to edges of pan.

Pour reserved olive oil evenly over top of dough. Sprinkle dough with reserved olive juice, reserved olive halves, rosemary and sea salt flakes. Set aside and allow to rest 15 minutes.

Dimple top of dough with bottom of spatula or fingertips and place in a cold oven. Set temperature to 350°F and bake for 35-40 minutes (350°F) or until golden brown.

Brush with olive oil and serve warm.

Tips & tricks

Baking this recipe in a cold oven to start, saves rising time. As the oven heats the dough rises in the oven.
