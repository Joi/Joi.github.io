Chicken Avocado Pupusas

Chicken

Prep Time: 25min Cook Time: 1h Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 portions Source: cookidoo.thermomix.com

Ingredients

Dough

7 oz masa harina

1 oz all-purpose flour

1 tsp baking powder

1 oz canola oil

9 oz water

½ tsp salt, to taste

Filling

35 oz water

1 carrot, cut into pieces (1 in.)

3 oz red new potato, cut into pieces (1 in.)

2 oz leek, cut into pieces (1 in.)

3 sprigs cilantro, leaves and tender stems

4 tsp chicken stock paste

1 oz extra virgin olive oil

12 oz chicken, boneless, skinless, cut into pieces (1 in.)

2 oz red onion, cut into pieces

4 - 5 oz avocado, cut into pieces (approx. 1 sm.)

1 oz mayonnaise

1 pinch salt, to taste

1 pinch freshly ground black pepper, to taste

8 tbsp cotija cheese

Directions

Dough

Place masa harina, flour, baking powder, vegetable oil, water and salt into mixing bowl and knead 20 sec/. Transfer to a bowl, cover with cling wrap and set aside. Clean mixing bowl.

Filling

Place water, carrot, potato, leek, cilantro, chicken stock paste and olive oil into mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 5.

Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl, weigh in chicken and poach 20 min/Varoma/speed 1. Remove simmering basket with spatula, remove chicken broth and reserve for another use (see Tip). Place poached chicken into mixing bowl and shred 4 sec//speed 4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

Place red onion into mixing bowl and chop 3 sec/speed 5. Transfer into bowl with shredded chicken.

Place avocado, mayonnaise, salt and pepper into mixing bowl and blend 3 sec/speed 5. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl with spatula and blend a further 2 sec/speed 5. Combine with chicken and onion mixture, transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate until ready to use.

Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Divide dough into 8 pieces and form into balls while pressing to form discs (approx. 3½ in.). Run a wet finger around edges to smooth any cracks. Place rounds on prepared baking sheet and cover with a kitchen towel.

Lightly brown rounds on medium heat in a non-stick pan and place them on prepared baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes (400°F).

Split pupusas open with a bread knife without completely separating them. Place 1 tbsp cotija cheese and 2 tbsp chicken filling in each shell. Serve at room temperature.

Tips & tricks

Add 3 sprigs cilantro leaves.

Add a pinch of cumin and a squeeze of lime to the reserved chicken broth and serve later as a vegetable soup.

For a crispier pupusa fry over medium high heat with 2 tbsp oil until golden brown.
