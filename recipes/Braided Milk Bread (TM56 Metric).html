Braided Milk Bread (TM5/6 Metric)

Bread and Biscuits

Prep Time: 20min Cook Time: 2h 20min Difficulty: Medium Servings: 30 slices Source: cookidoo.thermomix.com

Ingredients

50 g unsalted butter, in pieces, plus extra to grease

300 g milk

2 tsp dried instant yeast

20 - 60 g sugar, to taste

550 g bread flour

1 ½ tsp salt

1 large egg, lightly whisked, for glazing

Directions

Lightly grease a large bowl and set aside. Place milk, butter, yeast and sugar into mixing bowl and warm 3 min/37°C/speed 2.

Add flour and salt and knead 3 min/. Place dough into prepared bowl, cover with cling wrap or a damp kitchen towel and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 1 hr.).

Line a baking sheet (approx. 40 cm x 35 cm) with baking paper and set aside. Divide dough into 3 pieces and roll each piece into a rope (approx. 45 cm). Join the 3 ropes at one end, braid together, and join again at the other end. Place braided dough onto prepared baking sheet, cover with cling wrap or a damp kitchen towel and let rise again in a warm place until doubled in size (approx. 30 min.). Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350°F/180°C.

Brush braid with whisked egg and bake for 25-30 minutes (350°F/180°C) or until bread sounds hollow when tapped on the underside. Allow to cool on a wire rack before slicing.

Tips & tricks

The dough will be slightly sticky. If dough seems very wet after kneading, add 1 tbsp bread flour and knead a further Dough/3sec.

If living at high altitude let dough rise longer than recipe suggests. A mesh pop-up food cover can be placed over bread dough for the second rise. Lay hot, wet tea towels over top and sides of mesh food cover to stop dough from drying out while rising. Alternatively use the proof function on your oven.

Sprinkle with flaked almonds or turbinado sugar after brushing with egg.
